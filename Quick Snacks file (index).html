What Is Snacks?

A snack is a small portion of food generally eaten between meals.[1] A snack is often less than 200[2] calories, but this can vary. Snacks come in a variety of forms including packaged snack foods and other processed foods, as well as items made from fresh ingredients at home.

"Evolution of Snack Foods: From Homemade to Packaged Convenience"

Traditionally, snacks are prepared from a number of ingredients commonly available at home without a great deal of preparation. Often cold cuts, fruits, leftovers, nuts, sandwiches, and sweets are used as snacks. With the spread of convenience stores, packaged snack foods became a significantly profitable business.

#Categories

Snacks    Categories

Chips

Chips, often synonymous with crispy and salty indulgence, have become a staple snack worldwide. Whether made from potatoes, corn, or even vegetables, these thinly sliced and fried treats offer a satisfying crunch that appeals to a wide range of palates.

Nuts

Nuts are a nutrient-dense snack that offer a blend of healthy fats, protein, and essential vitamins and minerals. Whether it's almonds, walnuts, cashews, or pistachios, nuts are celebrated for their heart-healthy benefits and their ability to provide sustained energy.

Namak Paray

Namak paray are a popular South Asian snack, known for their crispy texture and savory flavor. Made from a dough of flour, salt, and a hint of spices, these bite-sized treats are deep-fried until golden brown, creating a satisfying crunch in every bite.

Crackers

Crackers are a versatile and crunchy snack that can be enjoyed on their own or paired with a variety of toppings. Typically made from flour and baked to a crisp, seasoned, or even cheese-filled—offering something for every taste.

Pizza

Pizza is a globally beloved dish that combines a crispy, chewy crust with a rich layering of tomato sauce, cheese, and a variety of toppings.

Noodles

Noodles are a versatile and beloved staple in many cuisines around the world, known for their comforting texture and adaptability to a wide range of flavors.

Kuluban Salad

Kuluban is a traditional[1] salad of Central Java. Kuluban can be consumed on its own as a salad for vegetarian meals or as a side dish. Kuluban is one of ancient Javanese cuisine, as it was mentioned in inscription dated from Medang Mataram era circa 9th century CE.[2].

Lawar (food)

Lawar (Balinese: ᬮᬯᬃ) is an Indonesian dish created from a mixture of vegetables, coconut, and minced meat mixed with rich herbs and spices, originating from Bali, Indonesia. This dish is commonly found in restaurants and warungs in Bali. Despite its rich vegetable mixture, lawar is not a vegetarian dish, since most often it is mixed with minced meat or even blood.[1]

Urab Sayur.

Urap (sometimes spelled urab or in its plural form urap-urap) is a salad dish of steamed vegetables mixed with seasoned and spiced grated coconut for dressing.[1] It is commonly found in Indonesian cuisine, more precisely Javanese cuisine. Urap can be consumed on its own as a salad for vegetarian meals[2] or as a side dish. Urap is usually found as a prerequisite side dish of Javanese tumpeng, a cone-shaped rice mound surrounded with assorted dishes, as well as part of a nasi kuning dish. In Balinese cuisine, it is known as Urab sayur..
